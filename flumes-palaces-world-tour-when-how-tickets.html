Flume's Palaces World Tour: When is it and how can I get tickets?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, September 7, 2024

FLUME, or Harley Edward Streten, is an Australian musician, DJ, and record producer, who has been active since 2010.

While he was relatively quiet in 2021, Flume has announced that he will be going on a world tour to celebrate the release of his album Palaces.

When is it and how can I get tickets?

The Stage 1 Palaces World Tour dates were announced by the Australian musician, with the first night of the tour taking place on April 14 in Las Vegas.

Between appearances at Coachella and Bonnaroo, the North American leg of the tour will take place. 

Flume will be accompanied by an all-star ensemble of opening performers for these headline performances, including Floating Points, Jon Hopkins, Tinashe, Channel Tres, Magdalena Bay, and Jim-E Stack.

On Friday, February 15 at 10am local time, a ticket pre-sale will commence, followed by a general on-sale on February 18 via Ticketmaster. 

Fans may sign up for early access to tickets on Flume's website.

Who is Flume?

Born on November 5, 1991, Flume, debuted his self-titled album to excellent reviews on November 9, 2012, topping the ARIA Albums Chart and earning double-platinum certification in Australia. 

Flume is widely considered a future bass pioneer who helped popularize the genre. 

Lorde, Sam Smith, Arcade Fire, Hermitude, and Disclosure have all had songs remixed by Flume. 

Skin, his second studio album, was released on May 27, 2016, to generally excellent reviews, and it once again topped the ARIA Albums Chart. 

At the 2017 Grammy Awards, the album was named Best Dance/Electronic Album. The album's debut song, Never Be Like You, received international attention after being nominated for Best Dance Recording at the Grammy Awards.

Following the release of Skin, Flume released the album's B-side EPs, Skin Companion EP 1 on November 25, 2016, and Skin Companion EP 2 on February 17, 2017. 

Hi This Is Flume, a mixtape he released on March 20, 2019, also received favorable reviews.

Palaces, his newest album, will be his first album released in six years on May 20.
