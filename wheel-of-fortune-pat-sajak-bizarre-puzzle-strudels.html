Wheel of Fortune host Pat Sajak admits bizarre new puzzle is 'silly' as fans bash the clue over its

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Saturday, August 3, 2024

PAT Sajak has admitted that a Wheel of Fortune puzzle on Tuesday's episode was not their best moment.

The contestants were asked to solve for "Noodles and Strudels" - fans agreed after he confessed it was "silly."

The Wheel of Fortune episode was won by Raul who won the one-contestant bonus round and spun off with a nice $59.9K.

However, it was Luci who solved the particularly odd puzzle earlier on.

The category was "Rhyme Time" and after a few letters appeared on the board, she seemed unsure as she rang in to guess it.

“Noodles and Strudels?” Luci asked in bewilderment at her own solution.

Pat, 76, deemed the contestant correct but had to say: “What a silly thing to have to ask for on national television."

He justified: "But you know what? It rhymes!"

'STRUDELS?'

One fan accused on Twitter: "Strudels is not a word."

Another wrote: "So I’m here now eating a pop tart and we just watching Wheel of Fortune lol."

PUZZLING

Last week, Young-Mei went to the final round with the highest score of $22K and lost out on a car over a puzzle fans deemed "unfair."

Yung-Mei instantly looked bewildered by what the third-word "Thing" could be, but wisely chose a "P" as well as an "C, "B" and an "A" tilting her head in confusion.

Yung-Mei didn't have much in the way of guesses as the 10-second timer ran out.

After "A Peculiar Quirk" showed itself she simply exclaimed: "Oh jeez."

The contestant threw her hands up in frustration as Pat said: "It was a quirk, quirk."

A pin drop could be heard in Stage 11 studios as the player added: "Huh."

Pat opened the prize card and revealed: "You could have won the MA - sorry the VW" as he was holding it upside down, which was funny at least.

Yung-Mei solemnly replied: "That's great thank you" and shook his hand

'REALLY?'

Fans were just not buying this one and felt having the last word of the one-person special solve start with a "Q" was a stretch.

Many also claimed "A Peculiar Quirk" didn't really qualify as a "thing" as it's not an object.

One wrote to Twitter: "REALLY!!! A peculiar Quirk!!! That’s ridiculous what are you over budget or something nobody would get that in their lifetime I’ve been watching for 35 years and get 75% that’s just BS."

Two weeks ago, after three contestants similarly won three bonus puzzles in a row, Thursday's winner was asked to guess something ridiculous as well - "Foamy Milk."

Keith looked flat-out furious and one angry viewer accused there was a pattern on the board.

"Keith got jived, BS. Foamed Milk?? REALLY. We know you gave out a ton of cash this week - that’s a crock of s**t come on ya freaking robbed the guy. Keith, you did awesome."

Meanwhile, the puzzles before the special bonus round on the show are often also a stretch lately.

Last month, the long-running show asked the contestants to solve a Holiday-puzzle as "Buttery chestnuts" which fans said was odd and "PG13".
