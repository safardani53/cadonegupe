Britton Colquitt Brother: All About Dustin Colquitt! [2024 Update]

Posted by Jenniffer Sheldon on Monday, July 8, 2024

The older brother of American football punter Britton Colquitt, Dustin Colquitt, just like his brother, is a punter who plays in the National Football League but is currently a free agent.

Dustin comes from a family with an eminent lineage of punters, including his father, Craig, who played in the NFL from 1978 to 1984.

His father, Craig, played in the NFL for eight seasons, particularly with the Pittsburgh Steelers, and was a member of two Super Bowl championship teams with the Steelers in 1978 and 1979.

The Tennessee native Dustin has played for Pittsburgh Steelers, Atlanta Falcons, Cleveland Browns, and Jacksonville Jaguars, besides Kansas City Chiefs, who drafted him in the 2005 NFL Draft.

Besides his brother and father, his uncle Jimmy Colquitt also played as a punter in the NFL.

Britton Colquitt Brother | Early Life & Education

Dustin Colquitt was born Dustin Farr Colquitt in Knoxville, Tennessee, United States, on May 6, 1982, to Craig Colquitt and Ann Colquitt.

Colquitt’s extended family have been sportsmen.

His father, Craig, played eight seasons in the NFL as a punter for the teams such as Pittsburgh Steelers and Indianapolis Colts.

Similarly, his uncle Jimmy played two games for the Seattle Seahawks.

Seeing his father and uncle play in the NFL, Dustin developed a likeness for football as a kid. He always dreamt of playing in the national league.

Dustin attended Bearden High School in his native Knoxville and played football for Bearden Bulldogs.

Aside from football, he also excelled in soccer back in the day.

After high school, Dustin accepted an athletic scholarship to attend the University of Tennessee in Knoxville.

He played colligate football for the Tennessee Volunteers football team from 2001 to 2004 under the coach Phillip Fulmer.

Britton Colquitt Brother | Career

Dustin Colquitt has been active in the football scene ever since he was a kid.

He played college football for the Tennessee Volunteers football team at the University of Tennessee from 2001 to 2004.

Dustin was recognized as a first-team All-Southeastern Conference (SEC) selection as a junior in 2003, the honor he once again earned as a senior the following year.

After college, Dustin was drafted in the third round of the 2005 NFL Draft and was soon signed in a three-year rookie contract.

During the 2007 season, Colquitt set the franchise record for the longest punt with 81 yards.

He played with the Chiefs for 15 successful seasons and won his first Super Bowl during the 2019 season after the Chiefs defeated the San Francisco 49ers.

Dustin also became the highest-paid punter during his time with the Chiefs.

On April 28, 2020, he was released by the team.

He then signed one year contract with the Pittsburgh Steelers on September 2020.

However, the team released him in October same year.

Colquitt then signed as an active roster or practice squad to a bunch of different teams, ultimately signing with the Browns on December 10, 2021.

He is currently a free agent.

Short Bio On Britton Colquitt

Dustin’s brother Britton Colquitt is a football punter who is a free agent. Just like his brother, he was born in Knoxville, Tennessee, on March 20, 1985.

He went to Bearden High School, where he played football and basketball.

After graduating high school, Britton enrolled in the University of Tennessee and played football as a punter for Tennessee Volunteers.

With the Tennessee Volunteers, he earned consensus first-team All-SEC honors in 2006. He was not just active in sports but also a scholar, as he majored in political science.

Britton was originally signed as an undrafted free agent by the Denver Broncos in December 2009. On December 22, 2009, Britton was signed to the practice squad of the Miami Dolphins.

However, eight days later, he was signed to the active roster of the Denver Broncos.

Following that, Colquitt was signed by the Cleveland Browns on a one-year contract in 2016 and by the Minnesota Vikings on September 1, 2019.

He was released by the Vikings on September 1, 2021, after the team signed Jordan Berry.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQS)

Who is the highest-paid punter in the NFL?

Sadly, the highest-paid punter in the NFL is not one of the Colquitts.

On October 2022, Jack Fox signed a three-year extension with the Detroit Lions, making him the highest-paid punter in the NFL.

What record does Dustin Colquitt hold?

Dustin set a Kansas Chiefs franchise record for most games played in 2019. He surpassed former teammate Will Shields and still holds the record.

Which family has the most NFL players?

It’s not the Mannings or the Colquitts. The family with the most NFL players is the Matthews family, who have produced seven professional football players spanning three generations.
