What is a designer vagina and how much does labiaplasty cost? The US Sun

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Sunday, June 23, 2024

THERE are no limits when it comes to cosmetic surgery.

Having a so-called "designer vagina" has been growing in popularity among women. But what is it?

What is a designer vagina?

A designer vagina is one of the fastest growing cosmetic surgery trends around the world which has been popularised by celebrities.

The procedure's actual name is labiaplasty, which involves altering the size of the labia minora.

It can be for both aesthetic reasons or for practical reasons, such as, the extra tissue is causing discomfort during sex or exercise.

This type of surgery should not be done on girls younger than 18 because the labia continues to develop beyond puberty into early adulthood.

It involves shortening or reshaping the vaginal lips with the unwanted tissue cut away using a scalpel or laser.

The loose edge may then be stitched with fine, dissolvable stitches.

From start to finish, the operation takes around one to two hours and usually patients can go home the same day.

What is vaginoplasty and labiaplasty?

There are two different types of surgeries when it comes to designer vaginas.

Labiaplasty reduces the size of the labia minora – which is the skin either side of the vaginal opening.

Vaginoplasty is designed to reduce the size of the actual vagina usually to make it tighter.

What is a non-surgical vagina facelift?

If you don't want to go under the knife there is the option of a non-surgical vagina facelift.

This involves a variety of different treatments from laser surgery to fillers and radiofrequency treatment.

The idea is to induce collagen tightening down below, usually after the area has been weakened from childbirth, through age or hormone changes.

Mum-of-five Lindsay Haitt told This Morning the treatment had “changed her life”.

She said: "My confidence came back, my sex drive came back, I just felt great.

“I felt like a virgin again. It just tightened everything up, it neatens it up to how you were before."

Many celebs have undergone cosmetic surgery or who have spoken out about considering the procedure.

From the Kardashians talking about getting laser vaginal rejuvenation to Sharon Osborne revealing she had a vaginal tightening procedure.

How much do the procedures cost?

Depending on where you go you can pay between £2,000 and £4,000 for the procedure plus the price of any consultations or follow up appointments.

For the non-surgical vagina facelift prices are around £499 per session, with many people opting for more than one visit.

Lyndsey Hiatt revealed she had paid £1,000 for two facelift procedures.
