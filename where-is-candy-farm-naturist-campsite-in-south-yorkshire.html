Where is Candy Farm? Naturist campsite in South Yorkshire allegedly filmed by police helicopter

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Friday, March 8, 2024

CANDY Farm is a nudist-friendly campsite that has been named in a trial involving a "sex-obsessed" cop who recorded people having sex from a Â£4million police helicopter.

Adrian Pogmore, 51, abused his position as an Air Observer when he flew over the naturist retreat and zoomed in on a naked couple sat outside their caravan.

Where is Candy Farm?

Candy Farm is between Doncaster and Scunthorpe in South Yorkshire, on the border with the East Riding of Yorkshire, Nottinghamshire and Lincolnshire.

It is situated close to the village of Wroot in North Lincolnshire and is not far from Doncaster Sheffield Airport.

What is Candy Farm?

Candy Farm is a naturist retreat where nudists can shed their clothes in a safe and accepting environment.

According to the website: "You can sunbathe naked and watch the cows come home, go fishing, visit the local pubs, or go for quiet country walks with no hills to climb, amongst trees and lakes."

Who is Adrian Pogmore?

Adrian Pogmore, 51, pleaded guilty to using hi-tech camera on board the police helicopter to record seedy footage of his "swinging friends" having sex.

He filmed his pals romping on their patio for eight minutes.

The Air Observer  also took footage of a nude mum-of-three and her teenage daughters sunbathing in their garden - as his crew mates allegedly watched.

Serving South Yorkshire officers Matthew Lucas, 42, and Lee Walls, 47, retired South Yorkshire Police pilot Malcolm Reeves, 64, and former South Yorkshire pilot Matthew Loosemore, 45, are currently on trial for being a part of Pogmore's set ups.
