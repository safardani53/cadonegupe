Can you beat the banker in Sun Bingos Deal or No Deal bingo rooms?

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Thursday, June 6, 2024

DEAL or No Deal was an iconic daytime TV show on Channel 4 from 2005 to 2016. While it might not be on TV screens, you can still get your DOND fix on Sun Bingo.

Over the 11 years that Deal or No Deal was active, 2975 shows aired.

Originally, players could win between 1p and £250,000, based on what amounts were in the boxes selected from the 22 boxes in play.

The first winner of the 1p prize was Nick Bain in 2006.

It wasn’t until 2007 that the first £250,000 prize was won, by Laura Pearce.

The game format changed in 2014 when Box 23 was introduced.

This new box was offered at the end of the game. The host at the helm, Noel Edmunds, asked the contestant whether they wanted to buy the special blue (rather than iconic red) box for the amount they had already won on the show.

Box 23 contained one of five labels:

  • Double – This doubled the amount that the contestant would win, changing the potential winning pot to between 2p and £500,000.
  • +10,000 – This added £10,000 to the winning amount, allowing contestants to win between £10,000.01 and £260,000.
  • Money Back – The contestant was refunded the amount they paid for the box.
  • Half – The contestant was awarded half the amount they had secured during the game – making it possible to win between 0.5p and £125,000.
  • Nothing – The harshest possible outcome. The contestant would leave with nothing. Not even a chance to join the infamous 1p Club.

In the same year, the game introduced the Offer Button.

This option was available to contestants who correctly guessed the Banker’s opening offer within 10% of the actual offer.

The button could be pressed any time after the first five boxes had been opened. It meant the Banker had to make an instant offer.  

By the time the show stopped airing, 52 people had joined the 1p Club, and 9 lucky winners had scooped the £250,000!
