We review the Vauxhall Adam Rocks Air from price to economy and all its features

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Friday, July 26, 2024

VAUXHALL says the new Adam Rocks Air was inspired by Parkour athletes — the dudes who run, jump and somersault off buildings through city centre streets.

I'm not sure how that influences car design but Vauxhall is jumping for joy with another concrete jungle winner on its hands.

It is billing the Adam Rocks Air as three cars rolled into one — city car, SUV and cabrio all wrapped in one funky Crossover Utility Vehicle (CUV).

It certainly hits the mark in everything bar the cabriolet bit — it's more a glorified sunroof rather than proper wind-in-the-hair job.

The Adam has already been a success for Vauxhall. It was a totally modern rival to the Mini and Fiat 500 and many said it wouldn't work. But it has, with 13,000 sales in the UK so far and 100,000 across Europe.

I was the first UK journalist to drive the Adam Rocks Air in the shadow of Mount Snowdon this week — and I expect it to hit similar heights to the Adam.

The Rocks Air — which will also get a hardtop Rocks model next year — adds more tricks to the range.

There's simply nothing like it on the road. Only the Fiat Panda Cross, Mini Paceman and VW Cross Polo get close but they don't have the full panoramic sunroof or vast personalisation options.

It's on sale later this month with prices starting at £14,695. But for all its good looks, the best bit is under the bonnet with the new 1litre three-cylinder Ecotech engine. That is the best buy and will set you back £16,695.

FIRST IMPRESSIONS

Three-in-one … Vauxhall Adam Rocks Air

It’s an Adam that looks like it’s done a Jodie Marsh body-building programme. But more attractive.

Sales of the standard Adam are split 60/40 in favour of the ladies. Like the Mini and Fiat 500, I struggle to see how a bloke can get away with driving an Adam. But the Rocks Air is far more masculine and that was the aim of Vauxhall’s designers.

What immediately stands out is the higher and wider looks and raised ride height of 15mm. The full SUV-look machismo makeover is completed by anthracite-coloured cladding wrapped around the entire body.

The front trapezoidal grille, with a chrome central brand bar, is embedded in the cladding, while contrasting, satin chrome 'boomerang' elements encase the front fog lamps/indicator housings and the reversing/fog lamps at the rear.

An integrated rear roof spoiler and LED daytime running lights come as standard. Silver skid plates and a bold chrome exhaust complete the look.

There's a choice of standard 17in or optional 18in alloys.

These can be further personalised with new wheel clips in 'Wild' design and different colours that are unique to Rocks Air.

DRIVE TIME

Refined drive … Vauxhall Adam Rocks Air

This is the secret weapon — the 1litre Ecotech three-cylinder engine. Most threecylinder eco units slash fuel consumption but add noise and a gruff performance.

The Ford EcoBoost version is an example, but I still like it a lot — a bit like the old VW Beetle noise.

Remarkably, this new Ecotech has all the economical benefits but is remarkably quiet and refined.

Tons of boring technology has gone into the new engine but the end result is very impressive and it manages 0-62mph in 9.9 seconds and a top speed of 121mph.

It feels and drives with the undemanding poise, power and performance of a much bigger, more expensive car. Yet it still will reach around 55mpg and with just 119g/km of CO2. There are bigger engines which are less expensive to buy — a 1.2 and 1.4litre — but the 1litre is the pick of the pots.

INSIDE STORY

Funky … colourful interior

It's the same story as the standard Adam — bright and cheerful with lots of personlisation options.

The electric folding canvas roof is operated via a button in the front area of the headliner and folds back neatly on to the C-pillars in just seven seconds.

It can be opened or closed while the car is moving at up to 85mph. Although it's only really a glorified sunroof, the benefit is that it doesn't impact on boot space like a full cabriolet roof.

As with the other Adam models, the Rocks Air has the £275 IntelliLink infotainment system option which allows to connect your own smartphone and benefits like satnav. It also provides USB and Bluetooth connectivity, audio streaming, a 7in high-definition colour touch-screen and seven speakers.

The Rocks Air is the only car of its size to get cruise control fitted as standard with options of Advanced Park Assist (£450) with Blind Spot Alert and Winter Pack (£215) which includes heated steering wheel and seats. It's also the only car of its class to get Electric Power Steering as standard with dedicated City mode and Hill Start Assist also available as standard.

Key Facts

  •  Price: £16,695
  • Power: 1litre Ecotech
  • 0-62mph: 9.9 seconds
  • Top Speed: 121mph
  • Economy: 55mpg
  • CO2: 119g/km
