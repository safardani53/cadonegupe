Orlando Robinson: Family &amp; Net Worth [2023 Update]

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, July 2, 2024

Despite being undrafted in the NBA Draft, American basketball player Orlando Robinson’s hunger for prominence never ceased.

After training camps & G-league drills, he landed in NBA & currently plays for Miami Heat as a two-way player.

Orlando Robinson’s deal sees him splitting time between the Heat & its G-league affiliate Sioux Falls Skyforce for the 2022-23 season before being a restricted free agent in 2023.

The Fresno State alumnus declared for the 2022 NBA Draft forgoing senior year at college but didn’t get picked. But his zeal for the NBA dream was not over for him.

In the 2022 NBA Summer League, he played for Miami Heat & later got into an Exhibit 10 contract that would follow his NBA debut with a two-way contract.

Robinson’s basketball passion comes from his father, a former college basketball player who later trained him.

With new NBA fame, people are also prying eyes on Orlando’s personal and private life.

Early Life & Family Of Orlando Robinson

Robinson Jr. was born on July 10, 2000, in Las Vegas, Nevada, to Orlando Sr. and Christa Fells.

His father is a former college basketball player who played for CSU San Bernardino.

Currently, Orlando’s father works as a union pipefitter. The NBA player not only inherited the sportsmanship from his dad but also a physique; his father is 6 feet 5 inches (1.95 m) tall.

The basketball player’s mother, Christa, is a teacher-turned-firefighter at Clark County Fire Department.

A 2001 graduate of the University of Nevada- Las Vegas, Christa Fells joined the Fire Department in 2013 & now also works as an instructor & fire engineer.

Both his parents seem supporting and loving toward the basketball player.

He has one sister, Aadiah Fells Robinson, who went to Centennial High School. Like her brother, Aadiah also plays basketball.

Age, Height & Scout Report

The Las Vegas, Nevada native is 23 years old. His zodiac sign is Cancer, and according to the Chinese Zodiac, he was born in the Dragon year.

The Heat power forward stands at 2.08 m ( 6 feet 10 inches) and has maintained an optimal weight of 235 lbs (107 kg). He has a wingspan of 7 feet 4 inches (2.23 m).

Although his NBA athletic ability is below average, his defense is strong because he puts in a reasonable effort and has worked to understand the subtleties of where to be and how to use posture and space.

Basketball At High School & College

Orlando attended three different schools: Centennial High School (until sophomore year), Cathedral High School (junior year), and Middlebrooks Academy (senior year).

Then, on October 11, 2018, the American basketball player committed to Fresno State; he received offers from 28 colleges including Oregon State, Georgia Tech, and Boise State.

During his freshman year, Orlando Robinson Jr. started in all 30 games & averaged 12.2 points, 6.6 rebounds & 1.6 assists per game.

On January 4, 2021, the NBA player scored his first-ever 30+ milestone with 33 points in a victory against Wyoming.

His sophomore year concluded with 14.6 points, 9.2 rebounds & 2.1 assists.

As a senior, he was named the 2022 First-team All-Mountain West, averaging 19.4 points, 8.4 rebounds & 2.9 assists.

Among his impressive games was against Wyoming on March 5, 2022, where he scored a college career-high 34 points.

Professional Career

Orlando Robinson Jr. went undrafted in the 2022 NBA Draft, joining the list of undrafted players: Jared Rhoden, John Butler, Scotty Pippen Jr. & Dominick Barlow.

On July 7, 2022, he signed an Exhibit-10 contract with Miami Heat & played for them in the 2022 Summer League, and then in pre-season. He was waived on October 13.

On November 13, 2022, Robinson inked a two-way contract with the Heat only to see a waiver on November 25 after two NBA games.

So, he would subsequently join its G-league affiliate Sioux Falls Skyforce & scored 20+ points in 7-out-of-10 games in Showcase Cup.

His NBA G-league career-high is 28 points against Motor City Cruise on December 9, 2022.

Later, he signed another two-way contract with the Heats on December 11, 2022. 

Net Worth & Salary Of Orlando Robinson

Even though Robinson Jr. is yet to land a standard NBA contract, he indeed gathered a considerable sum with games in the G-league.

As of 2023, Orlando Robinson has an estimated net worth in the range of $300K- $500K.

After going undrafted in the NBA Draft, he signed an Exhibit-10 (non-guaranteed) contract worth $1.1 million with the Heat but they ended the deal.

While assigned to NBA G-league’s Skyforce, he saw a two-way contract two times; one from November 13-November 25, 2022 & then another on December 11, 2022.

As per NBA rules, the two-way deal allows a team to sign a player for a minimal salary for one year.

The two-way players are expected to earn flat half of the major league’s (NBA’s) minimum salary (which is $1 million). So, from the deal, Orlando could be earning around $500K.

In 2023, Robinson Jr. will be an unrestricted free agent & the Heat have put in a qualifying offer of $1.7 million.

For the 2022-23 season, the Heat’s three highest-capped players are Jimmy Butler, Bam Adebayo, and Kyle Lowry.
