New Zealand boss Anthony Hudson in frame to become new Norwich City boss to replace Alex Neil

Posted by Reinaldo Massengill on Saturday, September 7, 2024

ANTHONY HUDSON is in the frame to become the new Norwich City boss, SunSport understands.

The Canaries are looking for a new manager following the sacking of Alex Neil who fell out with the club board about the future direction of the club following a disastrous attempt to return to the Premier eagles at the first time of asking.

The young English coach is currently in charge of New Zealand and was strongly linked with the vacancy at Derby County before on Steve McClaren returned for his second spell last October.

The 35-year-old coach turned down the MK Dons job last year as the Kiwis gear up for the

Confederations Cup in Russia this summer after winning the Oceania Nations Cup.

SunSport have been old Hudson who is keen to return to English football if the right opportunity is available is on a five candidate shortlist that includes former England boss Roy Hodgson, Alan Pardew, Nigel Pearson and Alan Irvine.

Informal conversations have already taken place with Canaries Chairman Ed Balls and Hudson's representatives about taking over the reigns at Carrow Road.

Hodgson and Pardew look to be the front runners, Hudson who will be the first British boss to lead a team at the Confederations Cup feels he could be the man to take the East Anglian club back to the Premier League next season.

The Norwich hierarchy like Hudson's profile in terms of his age, experience of working abroad and his success with New Zealand.
