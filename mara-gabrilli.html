Career

Mara Gabrilli has achieved several achievments during her life. In 2018, she became the first Brazilian to serve on a United Nations Committee. While serving as a Federal Deputy, she received the Congress in Focus Award. She was also named Sao Paulo’s finest Federal Deputy. Veja Magazine recognized her as the third-best Parliamentarian 2011 in 2011. Mara Gabrilli was elected as the second-best councilwoman in 2008 while she was a councilwoman.
She publishes blogs for TPM magazine websites in addition to working as a lawmaker.

For seven years, she worked as a columnist. Some of Mara Gabrilli’s columnist thoughts were included in the book Intima Desordem – the greatest writings in TPM (Arx and Versar publishers). She also contributes to newspapers. Prior to establishing her NGO, she served as the first director of the Municipal Secretariat for Persons with Disabilities and Reduced Mobility (SMPED). As an active legislator, she has introduced 43 measures to the City Council that have contributed to improving the lives of persons with impairments or limited mobility.

Many of the 43 pieces of legislation have been accepted by the council. Mara Gabrilli has also been named the first woman to serve on the Board of Directors of the Chamber of Deputies and the National Congress. Mara has long served as a permanent member of the Permanent Commissions on Constitution, Justice, and Citizenship (CCJ). She also serves as a permanent member of the Committees on Social Affairs (CAS), Foreign Affairs and National Defense (CRE), and Transparency, Governance, Inspection and Control, and Consumer Protection (CTFC).
