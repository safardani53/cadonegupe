Davy Jones Biography

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, June 2, 2024

Davy Jones was an English actor, musician, singer-songwriter, and businessman. He was the lead vocalist in the band The Monkees. Likewise, he also became the part of television series of the same name,in 1966. 

Davy Jones' Early Life

David Thomas Jones was born on 30th December 1945, in Openshaw, Manchester, England. He was the youngest kid of Harry Jones and Doris Jones. He has two elder sisters Berly and Linda.

He belonged to the white ethnicity and his birth sign was Capricorn.

Davy Jones started acting in his childhood but stopped after his mom died. Instead, he pursued becoming a professional jockey. He even dropped out of secondary school to pursue his career as a jockey.

Davy Jones' Relationship History

Davy Jones is married three times in his life by far. He married his first wife Dixie Linda Haines in December 1968 but kept his relationship secret until his first daughter Talia Elizabeth was born.

His second daughter Sarah Lee was born on 3rd July 1971 and after four years, the couple got divorced in 1975.

Later, he married Anita Pliinger on 24th January 1981. He had two daughters from his second wife, Jessica Lillian, and Annabel Charlotte.

The couple divorced during the 30th Anniversary reunion tour of in 1996.

David Jones married his third wife Jessica Pacheco on 30th August 2009; Jessica is 32 years junior to him. Coincidentally, this was the third marriage for Jessica as well. On July 2011, Jessica filed for divorce against Jones, but the suit was dropped in October of the same year.

Davy and Jessica were still married when he died in February 2012. Pacheco was cut out from Jones's will, which Jones made before their marriage. Consequently, she did not legally receive any of the properties owned by her husband.

Davy Jones' Cause of Death

David Jones died of a severe heart attack from arteriosclerosis. After riding his favorite horses in the track, he complained of chest pain and was rushed to Martin Memorial South Hospital and was pronounced dead on 29th February 2012.

Songs

Davy Jones was an English actor and musician who was best known as a member of the pop-rock group The Monkees. Here are some of his popular songs:

  • "Daydream Believer" - This song was released in 1967 and became one of The Monkees' biggest hits. It was written by John Stewart of The Kingston Trio.

  • "I Wanna Be Free" - This song was released in 1966 and was one of The Monkees' early hits. It was written by Boyce and Hart.

  • "A Little Bit Me, A Little Bit You" - This song was released in 1967 and became a top 20 hit for The Monkees. It was written by Neil Diamond.

  • "Look Out (Here Comes Tomorrow)" - This song was released in 1966 and was featured on The Monkees' second album, "More of The Monkees". It was written by Neil Diamond.

  • "Valleri" - This song was released in 1968 and became a top 5 hit for The Monkees. It was written by Boyce and Hart.

  • "Girl" - This song was released in 1967 and was featured on The Monkees' third album, "Headquarters". It was written by Davy Jones himself.

  • "She Hangs Out" - This song was released in 1967 and was featured on The Monkees' album, "Pisces, Aquarius, Capricorn & Jones Ltd." It was written by Jeff Barry.

  • "Cuddly Toy" - This song was released in 1968 and was featured on The Monkees' album, "The Birds, The Bees & The Monkees". It was written by Harry Nilsson.
