George Harvey Presnell is an American actor and singer. He began his career in the mid-1950s as a classical soprano, singing with orchestras and opera companies across the United States. His career shifted from classical music to musical theater in 1960 after Meredith Willson invited him to star in her new Broadway musical, The Unsinkable Molly Brown. His performance "Leadville Johnny" was a huge success and he reprized the role in the 1964 movie version of the musical, winning the Golden Globe for his performance. Presnell continued to star in a number of other films throughout the 1960s, but by the early 1970s that aspect of his career came to a halt. From 1970 to 1995, he worked primarily as a musical performer at Broadway, West End and in touring productions in New York City. In the early sixties, Presnell saw a resurgence in a career in film that lasted until his death.
