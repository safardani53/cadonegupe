Mystery as leading jockey Sean Levey to miss Racing League final and potential 20,000 bonus for 'm

Posted by Larita Shotwell on Wednesday, August 28, 2024

RACING LEAGUE leading rider Sean Levey will miss the final night at Newcastle after he was stood down at Sandown on Wednesday.

The 34-year-old was taken off his single mount at the Esher track for medical reasons.

He was originally booked to ride Miss Down Under for Amanda Perrett but was stood down by officials.

Levey last rode at Yarmouth on Tuesday but the BHA have since told him he cannot ride until further notice.

He had three rides pencilled in for Newcastle on Thursday but won't be at the track.

Nicola Currie jumps in to ride Scampi (5.45) and Don't Tell Claire (6.45) while Yacowlef (7.45) is a non-runner.

Levey was also booked for six rides at Newbury on Friday but has now been replaced on all of them.

The news comes just a week after fellow rider Marco Ghiani failed a drugs test and could face a possible six-month ban.

Levey currently stands to win a £20,000 bonus as top jockey in the £2million Racing League series.

A member of Matt Chapman's leading Team London and The South, Levey is 59 points clear of closest pursuer Harrison Shaw.

The final night of the six-week Racing League competition will take place at Newcastle tonight.

Shaw cannot catch Levey as he has only two rides on the night.

But in a funny twist of fate Levey's team-mate Daniel Muscutt could leapfrog him at the top of the table because he has seven rides.

Saffie Osborne, riding for her dad's Wales and The West team, could also overtake Levey and pocket the £20,000 top jockey prize.

Jockeys get 25 points for winning a race, with £50,000 going to the winning team and a further £5,000 going to each of the winning team's jockeys.

Levey, a multiple Group 1 winner on the Flat and one of the sport's best riders, has earnings of more than £1.2million on the track this year alone.

Over the past five years he has made just shy of £5.7million from racing in Britain.
