Pete Ricketts’ Biography

Pete Ricketts was born in Nebraska City, Nebraska, on August 19, 1964. He is a devout Roman Catholic. He is the eldest of Joe and Marlene Ricketts’ four children. Joe Ricketts was a businessman, and Marlene was a teacher.
Joe launched First Omaha Securities, one of the first bargain stockbrokers in the United States, in 1975, after moving his family to Omaha. After acquiring TD Waterhouse in 2006, the company changed its name to Ameritrade, went public in 1997, and changed its name to TD Ameritrade. Ricketts and his siblings, Tom, Laura, and Todd, all attended Omaha’s Westside High School, where he graduated in 1982.
He received a BA in biology from the University of Chicago in 1986 and an MBA in marketing and finance from the University of Chicago in 1991.

Pete Ricketts Career

Pete Ricketts returned to Omaha after finishing graduate school, according to his career. He spent a year working for the Union Pacific Railroad and then as a salesman for a Chicago environmental consultant.
In 1993, he began working for his father’s company, starting at the call center for a few months before being promoted to a series of executive positions, eventually becoming the company’s chief operating officer during his father’s stint as CEO.
He indicated in a 2006 report that his net worth was between $45 million and $50 million. In 2007, he co-founded and became director and president of the Platte Institute for Economic Research, describing it as a “free market think tank.” He is a Knight of the Holy Sepulchre and a member of the Knights of Columbus.

Pete Ricketts Wife

Pete Ricketts married Susanne Shore in 2020. Ricketts married Susanne Shore in 1997. Shore, a native of Garden City, Kansas, grew up in Tulsa, Oklahoma, where she got a bachelor’s degree in English and an MBA from Oklahoma State University. She was a nurse at St. Joseph’s Hospital in Omaha at the time of her marriage to Ricketts. Ricketts and Shore are the parents of three children, Roscoe, Margot, and Eleanor. The pair is currently residing in the Government’s home.
