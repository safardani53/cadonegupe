M&amp;S launches pink gin cream in time for Wimbledon

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Monday, February 12, 2024

NAME a more iconic British pairing than strawberries and cream… Go on, we’ll wait.

With Wimbledon next week, excitement is growing for any excuse to tuck into this tasty desert, and now M&S have launched a limited edition pink gin-flavoured option just in time for the tennis championships.

And it’s not just any limited edition pink gin-flavoured cream, it’s Marks & Spencer limited edition pink gin-flavoured cream.

Extra thick cream has been blended with the brand's best-selling Think Pink Gin and is priced at £3.50 for a 200g tub.

Described as a "delicious extra-thick cream infused with pink gin and a little raspberry for a blush colour and light berry flavour," it's set to be a sell-out as the sporting tournament returns for two weeks from 1st July.

M&S Dairy Product Developer Rosie While commented: "Our Pink Gin Cream is set to be a game-changer this summer. Our customers love gin in everything so we couldn't resist adding their favourite tipple to our extra-special cream just in time for Wimbledon."

The chain's also expecting to shift 12 million individual strawberries during the two-week tournament.

Yesterday, we revealed the new chocolate orange gin from That Boutique-y Gin Company that fans think tastes like McVitie's Jaffa Cakes.

Advertised on the website, the makers promise a heady mix of chocolate cake made with orange essence in the batter.

If that sounds too rich it also has touches of peppery juniper to cut through the sweetness, and fresh clementine, bitter cacao and floral elements to give lightness to the mixture.

One happy customer wrote: “Finding this gin to be like a slow burner... the more I drink, the more I like it. I wasn't sure at first, couldn't really taste the orange or the chocolate, but after the next few glugs, I could taste jaffa cakes! Yum! This is now on my favourites list...”

The zesty tipple, which comes in a pretty bottle that would look great on your summer bar, retails for £29.95.

You can snap one up on Amazon.

Chocolate orange is the latest flavour to grace supermarket shelves but if that doesn’t tickle your taste buds there are plenty more on offer.

Back in May, we revealed cider aficionados Kopparberg had tried their hand at making the spirit and released a pink gin that tastes of strawberry and lime.
