Jeff Henderson | Early Life and Family

Jeff Henderson was born on the 19th of February in 1989 in North Little Rock, Arkansas, United States. His parents are Laverne Henderson and Debra Henderson. He is the youngest among five siblings.

Jeff developed an interest in sports in his early childhood. As a result, the Henderson siblings spent a lot of time playing football in the neighborhood.

Moreover, Jeff used to compete in youth football and even played for his school team.

Later, Henderson transitioned into the track and chose to remain in the sport after he recognized how well he was at it. By his senior year, he was fully committed to track and field.

In 2007, he became class 6A long jump champion by jumping a remarkable 23’11”. In the same year, he jumped 24’0” to break the meet of champs record.

Jeff Henderson | Track and Field Career 

Collegiate Career

After showing his abilities in school, he made a promising start to his collegiate career. In 2007, Henderson enrolled in the Hinds community in Raymond, Mississippi.

During a couple of years there, he had already become a collegiate track and field sensation.

In 2008 and 2009, the athlete was the winner of the national junior college athletic association title in the outdoor long jump. Similarly, he also won the indoor title in 2008 and in 2009.

Furthermore, the Olympian also won a national title in 4*100 meters.

Following Hinds, he transferred to Florida Memorial University in Garden City from 2010 to 2011. At college, Henderson maintained his success streak intact by winning a plethora of other competitions.

For example, he was the National Association of Intercollegiate Athletics (NAIA) national champion in the indoor 60-m dash. Similarly, he was an all-American in the men’s 100-m dash and men’s long jump.

But unfortunately, the talented athlete got into a disagreement with the athletic department of the college regarding the treatment of an injury.

Following that, the gold medalist transferred to Stillman College, Alabama.

He won the all-American status at NCAA (national collegiate athletic association) Division II men’s outdoor track and field championships by winning the 100-m dash and the long jump.

Pro Career and Olympics

After the Olympian graduated in 2013 with a degree in business administration, his pro career has just begun.

He moved to California, took up Al Joyner (a former gold medalist in the triple jump) as his coach, and sponsored Adidas. 

In 2014, Jeff was the winner of the long jump at the USA track and field outdoor championship. He continued his remarkable feat in the 2015 Pan American games.

As the 2016 Olympics approached near, Jeff started concentrating on a new achievement on his record. As he aced the trials, all the eyes were on the talented athlete.

In the qualification for the Olympic finale, Henderson jumped his and the Olympics’ best, a wind-aided 28-214, leaving everyone in awe.

He took the lead on his first jump for the finals, but subsequently, the other three competitors passed him. The situation looked tough, but the gold medalist did a fabulous 27-6 on his final attempt and won a gold. 

It was an overwhelming moment for the USA as it was the first gold in the Olympic long jump since 2004 Athens. To make the moment more emotional, Jeff dedicated his victory to his mother, who has Alzheimer’s.

After Olympics

The next big event after Rio Olympics was probably the 2019 Doha World Athletics championship. Jeff slipped to a silver medal in the final event while Tajay Gayle of Jamaica took the gold home.

There were barely any competitions in 2020 during the coronavirus pandemic. Jeff took it as an opportunity to prepare for the Tokyo Olympics. 

Although he was ready to bring the gold back this year, luck wasn’t on his side as he finished in the sixth place at 8.08m.
